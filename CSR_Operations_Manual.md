## Quick Rules (Read First!)

### ‚è∞ When You Can Call

```
‚úÖ YES: Between 8:00 AM and 9:00 PM (customer's local time)
‚ùå NO: Before 8:00 AM or after 9:00 PM
‚ùå NO: Sundays (unless business is open Sundays)

Best times to call:
‚Ä¢ Tuesday-Thursday
‚Ä¢ Between 10:00 AM - 4:00 PM
‚Ä¢ Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons
```

### üìû Who You Can Call

```
‚úÖ YES - You can call these WITHOUT special permission:
   ‚Ä¢ Business office phone numbers (landlines)
   ‚Ä¢ Business listed on Google Maps
   ‚Ä¢ Companies you find online

‚ö†Ô∏è MAYBE - Need to check first:
   ‚Ä¢ Mobile/cell phone numbers (even if it's a business)
   ‚Ä¢ You must check the "Do Not Call List" first

‚ùå NO - NEVER call these:
   ‚Ä¢ Anyone on the "Do Not Call List"
   ‚Ä¢ Anyone who said "don't call me again"
   ‚Ä¢ Phone numbers marked as "OPT-OUT" in your system
```

### üí¨ Text Messages (SMS)

```
‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT: Texting has STRICTER rules than calling!

You can ONLY send text messages to people who:
   1. Gave you written permission, AND
   2. Are NOT on the Do Not Call List

If you're not 100% sure = DON'T TEXT THEM
```

## Before You Call ANYONE

### Check Each Number Before Dialing

**For EVERY single call, do this quick check:**

```
1. Look at the phone number

2. Ask yourself:
   ‚òê Is this a landline or mobile number?
   ‚òê Is this number on the Do Not Call list? (Should already be removed)
   ‚òê Did this person ask not to be called before?
   ‚òê Have I already called them today?

3. Check the time:
   ‚òê Is it between 8 AM - 9 PM in THEIR time zone?
   
4. Check if they're in your system:
   ‚òê Look for notes like "DO NOT CALL" or "OPT-OUT"
   
5. If everything looks good ‚Üí Make the call
   If anything looks wrong ‚Üí SKIP this number
```

### Is It a Landline or Mobile?

**Why this matters:** Mobile phones have stricter rules!

**How to tell:**

```
Landline numbers usually:
‚Ä¢ Listed as "business" in your database
‚Ä¢ Show office hours
‚Ä¢ Answer with company name

Mobile numbers usually:
‚Ä¢ Start with mobile area codes
‚Ä¢ Marked as "cell" in your database  
‚Ä¢ Personal voicemail greeting

‚ö†Ô∏è When in doubt:
   ‚Üí If your system says "Mobile" = Treat it as mobile
   ‚Üí If unsure = Ask your supervisor
```

**Rules for Mobile Numbers:**

```
If it's a MOBILE number, you can only call if:
   ‚úÖ Your manager marked it as "Approved for calling"
   ‚úÖ The system shows you have "consent" to call them
   
If you don't see "Approved" or "Consent" = SKIP IT
```

## How to Make a Call

### Step-by-Step Calling Process

#### Step 1: Prepare

```
‚òê Have the customer's information on your screen:
   ‚Ä¢ Name
   ‚Ä¢ Company name
   ‚Ä¢ Phone number
   ‚Ä¢ Reason for calling
   
‚òê Have your script ready

‚òê Have a pen ready to take notes

‚òê Take a deep breath and smile (they can hear it!)
```

#### Step 2: Dial the Number

```
‚òê Click "Call" in your system OR dial manually

‚òê Wait for it to ring (don't hang up too quickly)

‚òê Let it ring 4-5 times before hanging up

‚òê If voicemail picks up ‚Üí Follow voicemail script (see below)

#### Step 3: When Someone Answers

**First 10 seconds are CRITICAL! Say this:**

```
"Hello, this is [YOUR NAME] calling from [COMPANY NAME].
May I speak with [CONTACT NAME] please?"

Example:
"Hello, this is Sarah calling from ABC Marketing.
May I speak with John Smith please?"

```

#### Step 4: If You Get the Right Person

**Follow your call script, but remember to:**

```
‚úÖ Use their name
‚úÖ Speak clearly and slowly
‚úÖ Be polite and respectful
‚úÖ Listen carefully to their responses
‚úÖ Take notes in your system
```

#### Step 5: Ending the Call

**Always end with:**

```
"Thank you for your time, [NAME]. Have a great day!"

Even if they said no, stay polite!
```

## What to Say During the Call

### Opening Script (First 30 Seconds)

**Use this template:**

```
"Hello, this is [YOUR NAME] from [COMPANY NAME].

I'm calling to speak with [CONTACT NAME] about [BRIEF REASON].

"Hello, this is Mike from Studio Solutions.

```

### If They Say "What is this about?"

```
"We help businesses like yours [BRIEF BENEFIT].
I wanted to see if this might be helpful for you."

"We help businesses like yours answer customer calls 24/7

helpful for you."
```

### If They Say "I'm busy"

```

```

```

   ‚Üí Write "OPT-OUT" in your notes

   ‚Üí Tell your manager to add them to the Do Not Call list

### If They Say "Take me off your list" or "Don't call again"

**This is VERY important!**

```

   for the inconvenience."

   ‚Ä¢ "OPT-OUT REQUEST"
   ‚Ä¢ Today's date
   ‚Ä¢ Time

4. Report to your manager before end of day

### Required Information You MUST Say

**During EVERY call, you must mention:**

```
‚úÖ Your name
‚úÖ Your company name  
‚úÖ Why you're calling
‚úÖ Your callback number (if they ask)

## How to Send Text Messages

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **WARNING: Text messages have VERY STRICT RULES!**  
> Breaking these rules = Big fines for the company!

### Before Sending ANY Text Message

**Check ALL of these:**

```
‚òê Is this person marked as "SMS APPROVED" in your system?
‚òê Did they give written permission to text them?
‚òê Are they NOT on the Do Not Call list?
‚òê Have they texted us before (showing they want texts)?
‚òê Is it between 8 AM - 9 PM their local time?

If ANY answer is NO = DO NOT SEND THE TEXT!

When in doubt, ask your supervisor!
```

### Text Message Rules

#### ‚úÖ What You MUST Include in Every Text

```
Every business text message MUST have:

1. Your company name
2. Why you're texting
3. How to opt-out

Example:
"Hi John! This is Sarah from ABC Marketing. We have 
a special offer for your business. Reply STOP to 
unsubscribe."

All three parts are REQUIRED!
```

#### üì± Text Message Format

**Good example:**

```
"Hi [Name]! This is [Your Name] from [Company]. 
[Brief message - 1-2 sentences]. 
Reply STOP to opt out."
```

**Real example:**

```
"Hi Lisa! This is Mike from Studio Solutions. 
We're offering 50% off AI assistants this month 
for new customers. Reply STOP to opt out."
```

### If Someone Replies "STOP"

**This is CRITICAL - Follow these steps IMMEDIATELY:**

```
1. DO NOT send them any more messages

2. Reply once (you're allowed one reply):
   "You've been unsubscribed from ABC Marketing. 
   You won't receive more messages."

3. Mark their record as "SMS OPT-OUT"

4. Report to your manager the same day

5. Never text them again (even if they call you later)
```

**Other opt-out words to watch for:**

```
If they reply with ANY of these, treat it as STOP:
‚Ä¢ STOP
‚Ä¢ QUIT  
‚Ä¢ END
‚Ä¢ CANCEL
‚Ä¢ UNSUBSCRIBE
‚Ä¢ NO
‚Ä¢ REMOVE

‚Üí Stop texting them immediately!
```

### Text Message Timing

```
Best times to send texts:
‚úÖ Tuesday - Thursday
‚úÖ 10 AM - 5 PM (their local time)

Avoid:
‚ùå Before 8 AM or after 9 PM
‚ùå Weekends (unless you have special permission)
‚ùå Holidays
```

### How Many Texts Can You Send?

```
Maximum per person:
‚Ä¢ 1 text per day
‚Ä¢ 3 texts per week  
‚Ä¢ If no response after 3 texts = STOP trying

Never bombard someone with messages!
```

## If Someone Complains

### Stay Calm!

**If someone is angry or complaining:**

```
1. Stay calm and professional
   Say: "I apologize for any inconvenience."

2. Listen to their complaint
   Don't interrupt or argue

3. Offer to remove them from your list
   Say: "I'll make sure you're removed from our 
   calling list right away."

4. Thank them
   Say: "Thank you for letting me know. Have a great day."

5. Mark immediately as "DO NOT CALL"

6. Write detailed notes about the complaint

7. Report to your manager ASAP (same day!)
```

Then:
‚Ä¢ Mark as "DO NOT CALL - DNC LIST"
‚Ä¢ Report to manager IMMEDIATELY

#### "Stop calling me!"

Then:
‚Ä¢ Mark as "OPT-OUT REQUEST"
‚Ä¢ Never call again

#### "I'm going to report you!"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ‚ùå Mistake #1: Not Checking the Time

**Wrong:**
Calling at 7:30 AM because "I start work early"

**Right:**
Always wait until 8:00 AM in the customer's time zone

**How to avoid:**
Set a reminder on your phone: "No calls before 8 AM"

---

### ‚ùå Mistake #2: Skipping the DNC Check

**Wrong:**
"I'll check the DNC list later" ‚Üí calls anyway

**Right:**
ALWAYS check before calling - no exceptions

**How to avoid:**
Make it the first thing you do every morning

---

### ‚ùå Mistake #3: Calling the Same Person Too Many Times

**Wrong:**
Calling the same business 3 times in one day

**Right:**
Maximum once per day, wait 2-3 days between attempts

**How to avoid:**
Check "Last Called" date in your system before dialing

---

### ‚ùå Mistake #4: Forgetting to Log Opt-Outs

**Wrong:**
Someone says "don't call me" but you forget to mark it

**Right:**
Mark "OPT-OUT" immediately in your system

**How to avoid:**
Do it right after the call while it's fresh

---

### ‚ùå Mistake #5: Texting Without Permission

**Wrong:**
"I have their phone number, so I'll just text them"

**Right:**
Only text if system shows "SMS APPROVED"

**How to avoid:**
Double-check SMS permission before every text

---

### ‚ùå Mistake #6: Using Wrong Phone Number

**Wrong:**
Accidentally calling from your personal phone

**Right:**
Always use the company calling system

**How to avoid:**
Never dial manually from your cell phone

---

### ‚ùå Mistake #7: Not Leaving Voicemails

**Wrong:**
Hanging up immediately when voicemail answers

**Right:**
Leave a professional 20-30 second message

**How to avoid:**
Have your voicemail script always visible

---

### ‚ùå Mistake #8: Being Rude When Rejected

**Wrong:**
Getting frustrated when someone says "not interested"

**Right:**
Stay polite: "Thank you for your time!"

**How to avoid:**
Remember: It's not personal. Stay professional always.

## FAQ for CSRs

### Q: What if I accidentally call someone on the DNC list?

**A:** Don't panic! Immediately:
1. Mark them as "DO NOT CALL"
2. Tell your manager right away
3. Write detailed notes about what happened
4. Learn from it - be more careful with your DNC checks

One accident won't get you fired, but repeated mistakes will.

---

### Q: Can I call businesses on weekends?

**A:** Maybe - ask your manager first! 

Generally:
- **Saturday**: Sometimes OK for businesses that work Saturdays
- **Sunday**: Usually NO (most businesses closed)

Default rule: Stick to Monday-Friday unless told otherwise.

---

### Q: What if someone asks "How did you get my number?"

**A:** Be honest!

Say: "We found your business listed on Google Maps / online directory / [wherever you actually found it]. Would you like me to remove you from our list?"

Never lie!

---

### Q: Can I text someone if they didn't answer my call?

**A:** ONLY if they're marked "SMS APPROVED" in your system!

Just because you can call them doesn't mean you can text them.

Texting has stricter rules!

---

### Q: What if I'm not sure if a number is landline or mobile?

**A:** Check your system first - it should show line type.

If still not sure:
1. Ask your supervisor
2. When in doubt, treat it as MOBILE (safer)
3. Only call if you have permission for mobile numbers

---

### Q: How many times can I call the same person?

**A:** General rule:

- Same day: Once only
- Same week: Maximum 2-3 times
- If no answer after 3 attempts: Wait a week
- If they never answer after 3 weeks: Stop trying

Quality over quantity!

---

### Q: What if someone says they're busy but doesn't say "don't call"?

**A:** Offer to call back!

Say: "I understand! When would be a better time to call you back?"

If they give a time: 
- Schedule it in your system
- Call back at that exact time

If they say "never" or "don't call back":
- Treat it as an opt-out request

---

### Q: Can I call from my personal cell phone?

**A:** NO! Never!

Always use:
- Company calling system (Twilio/dialer)
- Company-provided phone
- Approved phone numbers only

Why? Your personal number isn't registered properly and could cause legal problems.

---

### Q: What if the person I need to talk to isn't available?

**A:** Ask:

"When would be a good time to call back for [NAME]?"

Then:
- Make a note in your system
- Schedule the callback
- Call back at that time

OR offer:
"Can I leave a voicemail?" or "Can I send them an email?"

---

### Q: What if someone asks for my personal phone number?

**A:** Give the company number instead!

Say: "The best number to reach me is our main office line: [COMPANY NUMBER]. You can ask for me, [YOUR NAME]."

Never give out:
- Your personal cell phone
- Your home number
- Your personal email

## Appendix: Glossary of Terms

**Simple definitions of terms you'll hear:**

- **TCPA**: Big law about phone calls and texts
- **DNC (Do Not Call)**: List of people who don't want marketing calls
- **Opt-Out**: When someone asks not to be contacted
- **Landline**: Regular office/desk phone
- **Mobile/Cell**: Personal cell phone (stricter rules!)
- **CRM**: The computer system where you track customers
- **Caller ID**: What shows up when you call someone
- **Voicemail**: Recording system when someone doesn't answer
- **SMS**: Text message
- **Area Code**: First 3 digits of phone number (like 555 in 555-1234)
- **Time Zone**: Different times across USA (East, Central, Mountain, Pacific)
- **Consent**: Written permission to call or text
- **Compliance**: Following all the rules and laws
- **A2P 10DLC**: Registration system for business texting
